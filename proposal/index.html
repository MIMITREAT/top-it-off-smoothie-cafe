Local Knowledge

Vivere is based in Western Colorado — same market, same community. Relationship-Based

When you message about a change six months from now, Joe doesn't need to re-read a brief or re-learn your codebase. He built it. That standing knowledge is worth real money — it means faster edits, better judgment calls, and no "onboarding tax" every time you need something done.

SEO From Day One

Not Bolted On. Built In.

SEO isn't a plugin you install after launch. 11 — Brand Protection: Legal, Liability & Compliance

A food & beverage business carries real-world risk — allergic reactions, health claims, and customer data all create exposure if they aren't handled carefully on the website. This build includes a layer of protection that most small-business sites simply don't have, designed to reduce liability and present the brand responsibly. This is risk mitigation built into the site, not an afterthought.

ProtectionWhat It DoesWhere It Lives
Allergen & Cross-Contamination NoticeClearly states that products are made in a shared kitchen and may contact common allergens, and directs guests to inform staff — a standard, expected food-service safeguard.Footer (every page) + dedicated box at the bottom of the menu
Disclaimers & Privacy PageA full, dedicated legal page covering allergens, nutrition/health, liability, pricing, intellectual property, privacy, and governing law.legal.html, linked in the footer of every page
Nutrition / Health DisclaimerClarifies that "boosts" and nutrition references are informational only, not medical claims, and have not been evaluated by the FDA — protecting against health-claim exposure.legal.html
Limitation of LiabilityLimits responsibility for allergic reactions and adverse effects "to the fullest extent permitted by Colorado law," with an "as-is" website provision.legal.html
Privacy PolicyExplains that contact-form data is used only to respond to inquiries and is never sold — increasingly expected by customers and platforms.legal.html
Colorado Governing LawEstablishes the State of Colorado and Mesa County courts as the governing jurisdiction for the site's terms.legal.html

Clarity Without Over-Promising

Marketing copy can quietly create legal exposure. Absolute words like "100%," "always," and "guaranteed" can be read as binding promises — and a single off-spec cup can turn a slogan into a complaint. Every page was reviewed and reworded to stay confident and on-brand while avoiding unprovable guarantees:

BeforeAfter
"100% Real Fruit""Real Fruit · Blended Fresh"
"made with real fruit and nothing artificial""crafted to order with real fruit and simple ingredients"
"real fruit, never powders or syrupy shortcuts""we focus on real fruit and skip the powders and syrupy shortcuts"
"Real Fruit, Always""Real Fruit, Front and Center"
"Better-For-You""Lighter Options"
"Cold-pressed, made daily" · "Natural caffeine kick""Cold-pressed and made fresh" · "A little caffeine kick"

Menu labels such as "vegan" and "dairy-free" are presented as a general guide, with a clear instruction to confirm with staff — protecting the cafe if a recipe or supplier changes.

Important: The disclaimers and privacy language provided are a strong, professional starting template tailored to a Grand Junction, Colorado food business — not legal advice. We recommend a brief review by a licensed Colorado attorney before launch, and the legal page itself carries a visible note to that effect. Vivere can incorporate any wording your attorney prefers at no extra design cost.
